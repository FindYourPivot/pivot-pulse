From Annie's Desk

The Question that drives us.

Dear friends,

After nine years away from work, she attended a community meetup she almost didn't go to. That one decision led to two hackathon wins and, today, a role as an AI Engineer at Prudential.

Her capability was never the question. She simply needed direction, opportunity, and a community that believed in her. Every month, I meet people like Lini. Their stories remind me why Pivot Project exists—to help people find their next opportunity in a world of constant change.

How do we ensure AI expands opportunity for all rather than narrows it?

Pivot is laying the foundations for the workforce mobility infrastructure needed in the AI era—connecting career direction, targeted skills, mentoring, employers and policy into one reinforcing ecosystem.

Because no one can build it alone.

Every new partnership strengthens that ecosystem—creating opportunities that extend well beyond a single programme or cohort.

Through our work, one insight has become increasingly clear: the biggest barriers to workforce mobility are often the non-skill gaps that traditional training programmes overlook.

Skills matter—but they are only one part of the equation. A student entering the workforce, a teacher preparing the next generation, a woman returning after a career break, a mid-career professional adapting to AI, and an SME owner leading organisational change each face a different combination of confidence, career direction, networks, identity, practical experience, employer access and support.

We do not believe these challenges can be solved with a one-size-fits-all programme. AI gives us the opportunity to personalise workforce transitions at a scale that simply was not possible before.

We're building a future where technology opens doors, and people help one another walk through them.

Innovating What's Next

Beyond one-time training

We're shaping AI Fellows as a transition pathway, not a course: skills, real assignments, employer exposure, and sustained work opportunities.

Fellows are placed as AI Productivity Specialists or Business Architects — and we're designing the pathway so that relationship doesn't have to end at placement.

Over time, we want Fellows to be able to keep supporting multiple SMEs as ongoing, fractional advisers.

PIVOTPOINT™ aims to track what happens after graduation — so we can start building an honest picture of what actually drives durable workforce mobility, not just what happens on completion day.

WORKFORCE SNAPSHOT

ASEAN reaches an AI tipping point

The International Labour Organization published its first ASEAN-wide look at generative AI's labour impact on 8 July: 22.9% of total employment across the region — nearly 80 million workers — sits in occupations with meaningful exposure to generative AI.

Read the full analysis

Its central conclusion is that disruption is not inevitable. Outcomes will depend on how effectively workers, employers and institutions prepare for the transition.

42.2%

Singapore: Opportunity through preparedness

Singapore has ASEAN’s highest workforce exposure to generative AI and is also the region’s most AI-ready economy. The opportunity is ensuring preparedness reaches workers in exposed occupations.

2×

Women face greater exposure

Across ASEAN, women are more than twice as likely as men to work in occupations with high AI exposure, reinforcing the importance of inclusive workforce transition pathways.

PIVOT PERSPECTIVE

Global research tells us what is changing. Our work on the ground helps us understand how people successfully navigate that change. Combining both is how better workforce solutions—and better policy—are built.
